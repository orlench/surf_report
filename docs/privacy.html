Privacy Policy

Should I Go? Surf Conditions App — Last updated: March 2026

TL;DR: We use your location only to find nearby surf spots. We don't track you, sell your data, or share it with third parties.

What We Collect

  • Approximate location — Used solely to detect your nearest surf spot via IP-based geolocation. We do not store your location on our servers.
  • Precise location (optional) — If you grant location permission for the map feature, your coordinates are used on-device to center the map. They are not sent to our servers.
  • Push notification token — If you enable surf alerts, your device token is stored on our server to deliver notifications. It is deleted when you unsubscribe.
  • Spot feedback text — If you submit feedback to tune a spot's score, the text is stored anonymously (no user ID or account is associated).

What We Don't Collect

  • No account or login required
  • No personal information (name, email, phone)
  • No analytics or usage tracking
  • No advertising identifiers
  • No data shared with or sold to third parties

Local Storage

The app stores your preferences locally on your device:

  • Last selected surf spot
  • Weight and skill level (for board recommendations)
  • Recent custom spots from the map
  • Push notification subscriptions

This data stays on your device and is never uploaded.

Third-Party Services

The app fetches surf condition data from our API server (api.shouldigo.surf), which aggregates publicly available weather and ocean data from sources including Open-Meteo, MET Norway, and Windfinder. No personal data is sent in these requests.

Data Retention

Push notification tokens are retained only while the subscription is active. Spot feedback is stored indefinitely but contains no personally identifiable information. Deleting the app removes all local data.

Children's Privacy

The app does not knowingly collect any data from children under 13.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL. Continued use of the app constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
